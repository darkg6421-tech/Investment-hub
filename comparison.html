Comparison of Investment Options

Investment Option Features Benefits Use Cases
SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)
  • Invest a fixed amount regularly
  • Automated investments
  • Reduces market timing risk
  • Disciplined investing
  • Rupee cost averaging
  • Flexibility in investment amounts
  • Long-term wealth creation
  • Retirement planning
  • Building an emergency fund
SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan)
  • Withdraw a fixed amount regularly
  • Can be set up for specific time frames
  • Provides regular income
  • Flexible income stream
  • Helps in managing expenses
  • Reduces the need to liquidate assets
  • Retirement planning
  • Funding regular expenses
  • Supplementing income
STP (Systematic Transfer Plan)
  • Transfer a fixed amount from one fund to another
  • Helps in managing risk
  • Can take advantage of different investment opportunities
  • Gradual exposure to equity markets
  • Flexibility in allocations
  • Optimizes returns
  • Transitioning assets during market changes
  • Rebalancing portfolios
  • Investing in different asset classes
Lump Sum
  • One-time investment
  • No regular commitments
  • Higher potential for returns if timed correctly
  • Higher returns in bullish markets
  • Simplicity in management
  • Opportunity to invest at market lows
  • Windfall gains
  • Investment of large sums at once
  • Short-term investments
